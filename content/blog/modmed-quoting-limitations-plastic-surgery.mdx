ModMed is one of the most widely adopted EMR platforms in plastic surgery. Its clinical documentation, scheduling, and billing tools are purpose-built for dermatology and plastic surgery workflows. But there's one area where ModMed consistently falls short: patient quoting.

For practices that rely on ModMed for everything, this gap often goes unnoticed, until a high-volume consultation month reveals just how much time is lost building quotes manually, how many errors creep into fee calculations, and how many patients walk out the door without a quote in hand.

## What ModMed's Quoting Actually Does

ModMed includes basic fee schedule management and the ability to generate estimates. But the functionality stops well short of what a busy surgical practice needs. There's no dynamic calculator that adjusts for surgeon-specific fees, no instant PDF quote to hand a patient at the end of a consultation, and no way to track whether a quoted patient ever converted to a booking.

For a general practice seeing a handful of surgical consultations per week, this might be manageable. For a high-volume cosmetic surgery center, where consults run back-to-back and every patient expects a detailed, professional quote before they leave, it creates a bottleneck.

## The Real Cost of Manual Quoting

When quoting isn't automated, it falls to your front desk or patient coordinator to assemble each quote from scratch. That means looking up facility fees, anesthesia rates, surgeon fees, and implant costs separately, then manually calculating totals and formatting a document that looks professional enough to hand to a patient. At 5 to 10 minutes per quote, and 20+ consultations per week, you're losing hours every week to a task that should be instant.

More critically, when quotes take time to produce, patients leave without one. And patients who leave without a quote rarely come back.
