DrChrono has become a popular choice for plastic surgery and cosmetic practices looking for a flexible, iPad-friendly EMR. Its clinical tools, scheduling, and billing integrations are solid. But when it comes to patient quoting, one of the most conversion-critical steps in a cosmetic surgery practice, DrChrono's capabilities are limited by design.

This isn't a knock on DrChrono. EMRs are built to manage clinical records and billing workflows. Generating a professional, accurate, surgeon-specific patient quote is a different problem, and most EMRs, including DrChrono, treat it as an afterthought.

## What's Actually Available in DrChrono

DrChrono includes basic fee schedule management and can generate simple estimates through its billing module. For practices with straightforward, single-procedure consultations and stable pricing, this may be sufficient.

But plastic surgery practices rarely fit that profile. Multi-procedure consultations are common. Surgeon fees vary. Facility and anesthesia rates change. Implant costs need to be incorporated. Branded, patient-facing PDFs need to look professional enough to reflect the practice's standard of care. DrChrono's estimate module wasn't designed for any of this.

## The Integration Gap

DrChrono's API is robust, which is why practices that have integrated it with a purpose-built quoting tool see the biggest gains. Patient demographics and procedure details flow from [DrChrono into the quoting platform](/integrations/) automatically, no double entry, no transcription errors.

## What Practices Are Missing

Practices relying solely on DrChrono for patient quoting typically experience one or more of the following: quotes that take 10 to 15 minutes to assemble manually, pricing errors that erode patient trust, patients who leave the consultation without a quote in hand, and no visibility into quote-to-booking conversion rates.

These aren't unsolvable problems. They're the predictable result of asking an EMR to do something it wasn't built for.
