If you're weighing ModMed's built-in quoting against a dedicated quote builder, this is the deep-dive comparison. ModMed is one of the most widely used EMRs in cosmetic surgery, and its quoting tool covers single-procedure estimates well. Most cosmetic practices need more than that: multiple procedures, multiple surgeons, facility and anesthesia math, implants, and commissions. Below is the honest, feature-by-feature side-by-side so you can decide which fits your practice.

If you're on ModMed and just want the short version, start with our [ModMed quoting page](/modmed-quoting/).

## The Mommy Makeover quote test

Take a realistic case. A patient wants a Mommy Makeover with Dr. Koehler: breast augmentation ($5,500), tummy tuck ($7,500), and liposuction ($3,500). OR time is 4.5 hours. Two implants. This is an ordinary cosmetic quote, and it's a good stress test.

**In ModMed.** You add three procedure line items. You calculate the facility fee by hand from the 4.5-hour OR time and type it in. You calculate anesthesia the same way. You add the implant costs as separate lines. Then you generate the PDF. Realistic time: 12 to 15 minutes. The PDF is functional but template-locked, and there's no audit trail of the fee edits you just made manually.

**In MySurgeryQuote.** Select Dr. Koehler from the dropdown. His pricing loads automatically. Add the three procedures. The facility and anesthesia fees calculate themselves from the 4.5-hour time. Pick the two implants from the catalog. Generate the quote. Realistic time: about 90 seconds.

Same quote. Same numbers. One takes a quarter of an hour and invites arithmetic mistakes; the other takes a minute and a half and shows its math.

The difference isn't that ModMed can't make an estimate. It's that ModMed treats each fee as a number you supply, while a dedicated tool treats fees as formulas it runs for you.

Nothing about your ModMed workflow changes. The quote build moves to a tool that's actually designed for it.

Adding MySurgeryQuote to ModMed isn't about speed alone. It's the capabilities ModMed quoting doesn't have:

- **Branded PDFs** that look like your practice, not your EMR. The document the patient takes home and compares against other practices.

The pattern is consistent: ModMed handles records and billing; MySurgeryQuote handles the quote. Each does the job it's built for.
