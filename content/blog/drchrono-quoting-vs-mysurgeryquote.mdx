DrChrono is a solid EMR for general cosmetic practices. Its quoting feature is built for single-line charge entry, not for itemized cosmetic estimates with facility fees, anesthesia, and implants. If you're a cosmetic surgeon on DrChrono, you're probably working around it already. Here's what that workaround is costing, and what cosmetic quoting actually requires.

## What DrChrono's quoting feature does

DrChrono is built for in-network medical practices, where charges flow into insurance claims. Its quoting feature reflects that. It's essentially a charge-entry tool: a single charge line, a basic patient-facing estimate, and not much beyond it.

That design is fine for a dermatology visit or an in-network procedure with one CPT code. It's a poor fit for cosmetic surgery, where a single quote routinely combines a surgeon fee, a facility fee, anesthesia, implants, and additional fees, none of which DrChrono's quoting tool itemizes the way a cash-pay cosmetic patient expects.

## What a cosmetic quote actually needs

Take a real example: a second-stage augmentation with Dr. Koehler.

- Surgeon fee: $6,645
- Facility fee: $1,900 (calculated from 2.0 hours of OR time at a $950 base plus hourly rate)
- Anesthesia fee: $780
- Implants: $2,700 (Allergan Natrelle)
- Additional fees: $600
- **Grand total: $12,625**

In DrChrono's quoting feature, this collapses into one charge line. You lose the breakdown, and the breakdown is the point. When a patient calls back and asks "what's the facility fee?" or "how much of this is the implants?", a coordinator needs the itemized view to answer in seconds. A single charge line can't do that.

Cosmetic patients are paying cash and comparing practices. The itemized, professional quote is part of the sale. A flattened charge line undercuts it.
